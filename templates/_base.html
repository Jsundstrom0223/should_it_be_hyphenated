Should It Be Hyphenated?

The question of whether a compound should be hyphenated can be surprisingly tricky to answer. Hyphenation often depends on context and part of speech; some terms, for example, are hyphenated before but not after a noun. Others are hyphenated when used as nouns but not as verbs. In other cases, it may be best to forgo hyphenation (or to include it) to maintain consistency within a document. And Googling a hyphenation question may be more confusing than helpful, because different style guides and sources have different (and often contradictory) standards. That's why it's best to turn to your friendly neighborhood editor with any hyphenation questions. But if you don't have an editor handy, an app made by an editor is the next best thing.
