Should It Be Hyphenated?

The question of whether a term should be hyphenated can be surprisingly tricky to answer. Hyphenation often depends on context and part of speech; some terms, for example, are hyphenated before but not after a noun. Others are hyphenated as adjectives, but not as nouns or verbs. In other cases, it may be best to forego hyphenation (or include it) to maintain consistency within a document. And Googling your hyphenation question may not be much help, because different style guides and sources have different standards. That's why it's best to turn to your friendly neighborhood editor with any hyphenation questions. But if you don't have an editor handy, an app made by an editor is the next best thing.
