The Basics

How does it work?

Once you've entered a compound that you think may be hyphenated, one of two things will happen: You'll either receive an answer to your hyphenation question, or you'll be asked a quick question about your use of the compound.

You'll receive an answer right away in the following cases:

  • The Chicago Manual of Style includes a hyphenation rule that's directly applicable to your search term
  • The compound is in Merriam-Webster's Collegiate® Dictionary as a hyphenated compound (e.g., "well-being"), a closed compound (e.g., "hardworking"), or an open compound (e.g., "close call")

    • Otherwise, the app will look up each part of your compound in Merriam-Webster's Collegiate Dictionary and ask you to pick the definition that most closely matches your use of the term. In that case, you’ll receive an answer after you’ve made your selections.

    What is it?

    Should It Be Hyphenated? is a Flask app with a Python back end. The app relies on Merriam-Webster's Collegiate® Dictionary with Audio API and is hosted on Render. For details on the JSON responses returned by the API and the app's handling of those responses, see "The App and the API: A Note on Merriam-Webster's Collegiate® Dictionary with Audio API." For more extensive information on the API, see the Merriam-Webster Developer Center's FAQ page or JSON documentation.

    What else should I know?

    Should It Be Hyphenated? is a work in progress with a few minor limitations.

  • The app adheres to Chicago Manual of Style hyphenation standards and retrieves all definitions / spellings from Merriam-Webster's Collegiate Dictionary, one of the dictionaries recommended by the manual. When the Chicago Manual and the dictionary disagree on the hyphenation of a term, the app gives precedence to the manual.
  • Currently, it does not support search terms with more than one hyphen; nor does it differentiate between compounds that use a hyphen and compounds that use an en dash.
  • It does not return information on undefined entry words (i.e., words that are included in the dictionary without a definition or a link to another term with a definition).
  • Should It Be Hyphenated? ignores the parts of speech listed below. If a dictionary entry's functional label (part of speech) field includes one of those parts of speech, the app will omit that entry from the entry information it returns; however, it will still return the other entries for the search term.

    • Abbreviations
    • Auxiliary verbs
    • Biographical names
    • Pronunciation spellings
    • Symbols
    • Trademarks
