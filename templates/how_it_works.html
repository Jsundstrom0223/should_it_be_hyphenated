The Basics

How does it work?

Once you've entered a compound that you think may be hyphenated, one of two things will happen: You'll either receive an answer to your hyphenation question, or you'll be asked a quick question about your use of the compound.

You'll receive an answer right away in the following cases:

  • The Chicago Manual of Style includes a hyphenation rule that's directly applicable to your search term
  • The compound is in Merriam-Webster's Collegiate® Dictionary as a hyphenated compound (e.g., "well-being"), a closed compound (e.g., "hardworking"), or an open compound (e.g., "close call")

    • Otherwise, the app will look up each part of your compound in Merriam-Webster's Collegiate Dictionary and ask you to pick the definition that most closely matches your use of the term. In that case, you’ll receive an answer after you’ve made your selections.

    What is it?

    Should It Be Hyphenated? is a Flask app with a Python back end. The app relies on Merriam-Webster's Collegiate® Dictionary with Audio API and is hosted on Render.

    What else should I know?

    Should It Be Hyphenated? is a work in progress with a few minor limitations.

  • The app adheres to Chicago Manual of Style hyphenation standards and retrieves all definitions / spellings from Merriam-Webster's Collegiate Dictionary, one of the dictionaries recommended by the manual.
  • Currently, the app does not support search terms with more than one hyphen; nor does it differentiate between compounds that use a hyphen and compounds that use an en dash.
  • Merriam-Webster's Collegiate® Dictionary with Audio API enforces a rate limit of 1,000 queries a day.
